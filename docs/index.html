Just Google it

Search engines have become “one of society’s key infrastructures for knowing and becoming informed”. The term ‘Google it’ is slowly replacing ‘find out’. But how many Google users actually stop to think critically about the dominant search engine and the results it retrieves?

With this idea in mind, we — a Swedish IT librarian, a Chilean-Swedish visual storyteller with an activist bent, a German librarian interested in digital inclusion, and a Czech-Canadian jaded ICT4D researcher and free speech advocate (link to people.html) — decided to look at the academics who do just that and so we turned to Primo in search of academic writing offering critical interrogation, critique — or even outright criticism — of Google for our group project for the fall 2024 module of Datalogical Thinking, part of the Master’s Programme in Information Science: Digital Environments at the University of Borås.

Using the retrieved Primo search results we created a dataset of peer-reviewed journal articles and then an algorithm using pseudocode and Python to answer the following questions:

  • What are the keywords in our dataset? What are the top 5 keywords in our dataset?
  • How many articles in our dataset include these top 5 keywords? What are their titles?
  • How many authors in our dataset have written on these top 5 keywords? What are their names?

>As we explain in the discussion, we added a fourth and final step, we asked the algorithm to create a word cloud from the descriptions of all articles in our dataset.
